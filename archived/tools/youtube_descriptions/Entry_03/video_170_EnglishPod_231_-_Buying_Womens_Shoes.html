EnglishPod 231 - Buying Women's Shoes Conversation Mom:  Hi, excuse me Miss? I'm looking for a dress shoe. My usual pair that I've had for years have finally been stretched out of shape. They don't provide any support anymore. Salesgirl:  Sure, what kind of shoe are you looking for? We've got strappy sandals, sleek high heels, edgy pumps, or if you're looking for something a little more practical, we've got Mary Janes, ballerinas. Mom:  Show me some classic high heels, please. Salesgirl:  Ok, right this way. What color did you have in mind? Mom:  Black. Classic. Salesgirl:  Of course. We've got this style here that is very popular. Because it's an open-toe shoe, you can wear it any time of the year. They look great on everyone. Mom:  Umm. too shiny. And I wear pantyhose with my shoes so let's look for a closed-toe shoe. Salesgirl:  Ok, these are a very nice pair of leather shoes with a two-inch heel so they are very comfortable. Mom:  I don't like the pointed toes. Let me take a look at what else you have. Too high. That one looks like the back would cut into my heel. I have a high instep so I doubt that one will fit properly. I don't want bows. I find slingbacks very uncomfortable. Those might as well be stilettos. Too modern. Ah, finally, this is what I'm looking for. Salesgirl:  What size? Mom:  Seven-and-a-half. Salesgirl:  Here we are How does it fit? Mom:  Hmmm. not good. They're too tight. The length is right, but the shoe is too narrow and it's pinching my toes. And there'd be no room for my insoles. You know what? I don't think I have the patience for this today. They just don't make shoes like they used to. I'll come back another time. Salesgirl:  Have a nice day, Ma'am. Key Vocabulary
  • Support: Something that holds a person
  • Strappy sandals: A shoe with a bottom part having straps
  • Edgy pump: A low-cut low-heeled shoe
  • Open-toe shoe: The shoe with the toes exposed
  • Two-inch heel: Length of the heel is 2 inches
  • Pointed shoe: The shoe having a narrow top
  • Pinch: Squeeze between the thumb and a finger
  • Insole: The inner sole of a shoe
  • Cut into: Tight and hurt the body with a sharp thing
Supplementary Vocabulary
  • Snug: Fitting closely
  • Loose: Not fitting closely or tightly
  • The (next) size up: One size bigger
  • The (next) size down: One size smaller
  • Break in: To wear or use in order to make comfortable
