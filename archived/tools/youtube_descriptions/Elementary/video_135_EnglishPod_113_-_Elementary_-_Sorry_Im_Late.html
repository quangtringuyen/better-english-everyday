EnglishPod 113 - Elementary - Sorry I'm Late Conversation A:  Where is everyone? We were supposed to start fifteen minutes ago! B:  Jo called and said she'd be here in a sec. She said she got tied up with a client. C:  Sorry I'm late everyone. There was a huge traffic jam on the highway this morning. D:  Morning everyone! Were you stuck in traffic as well, Jess? There was a huge pileup on the highway and traffic was backed up for miles. B:  Scott just called and said that he's running late. His last meeting ran over, but he's on his way now. A:  Guys, this is not acceptable. If I say the meeting starts at ten, the meeting starts at ten. Not ten-oh-one! And definitely not ten-ten! All right. Let's get started. So the first thing I want to talk about is our... E:  I'm really sorry, everyone! I know I'm late. But really, it's not my fault. I was getting a coffee at Starbucks, and the line was way too long. I was waiting for twenty minutes to get my coffee! Key Vocabulary
  • Backed up (verb): Blocked so you can't move forward
  • Get tied up (phrase): Became busy; unable to leave
  • Running late (verb): Going to be late
  • Run over (verb): To go beyond a limit
  • Stuck in traffic (phrase): So may cars on the road that you can not drive or move forward
  • In a sec (phrase): Soon
  • Huge (Adjective): Very big
  • Traffic jam (common noun, singular): A lot of cars stopped from moving
  • Pileup (common noun, singular): An accident where many cars crash into each other
Supplementary Vocabulary
  • Punctual (Adjective): The quality of always being on time
  • Behind schedule: Later than planed; late
  • Sleep in (verb): Sleep later than planned
  • Tardiness (Adjective): The quality of often being late
  • Hustle (verb): Move very quickly
