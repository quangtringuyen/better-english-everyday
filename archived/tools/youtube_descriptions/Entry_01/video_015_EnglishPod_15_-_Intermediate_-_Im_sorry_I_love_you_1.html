EnglishPod 15 - Intermediate - I'm sorry I love you 1 Conversation A:  Whoa, whoa, what's going on? Watch out! B:  Hey, watch where you're going! A:  Oh, no! I'm so sorry! Are you all right? B:  Oh...I don't know. A:  I feel terrible, I really didn't mean to knock you over. My tire, just exploded, and I lost control of my bike. Really, it was an accident. Please accept my apologies. B:  Just let me try to stand up. SONG:  Why do birds suddenly appear, every time you are near? A:  Are you okay? B:  Oh, wait a second, you seem really familiar, I think I know you from somewhere. A:  Yeah, I think we have met somewhere before. That's right! We met at Aaron's place last weekend! What a coincidence! But anyway, I'm glad to see that you're not too badly hurt, and I should probably get going. I have a nine o'clock meeting. B:  Ouch! My ankle! I think it's broken! You can't just leave me like this! Are you calling an ambulance? A:  Nope, I'm canceling my appointment so that I can stay here with you. SONG:  Do you remember when we met? That's the day I knew you were my pet. I wanna tell you how much I love you. Key Vocabulary
  • Feel terrible (phrase): Sorry
  • Mean (principle verb, present simple): Intend, plan
  • Explode (principle verb, past simple): Suddenly break apart
  • Accident (common noun, singular): An event that is not planed or intended
  • Familiar (Adjective): Probably known, but not clearly remembered
  • Place (common noun, singular): House
  • Coincidence (common noun, singular): Situation when two or more things happen at the same time in an unplanned way
  • Knock over (principle verb, infinitive): Make something fall over
Supplementary Vocabulary
  • Regret (principle verb, present simple): Feel sad or sorry about something
  • Apologetic (Adjective): Feeling or showing regret, showing an apology
  • Feel guilty (phrase): Feel responsible for doing something bad
  • Unfamiliar (Adjective): Not familiar
  • Love at first sight (phrase): Falling in love the first time you see someone
